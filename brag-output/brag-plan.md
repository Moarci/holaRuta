# Brag Plan: HolaRuta

## What is this app?
HolaRuta is a German-language travel-Spanish flashcard PWA for Latin America — 2,293 cards across 72 real travel situations (bus, food, money, border, emergency), with spaced repetition, three learn modes, fully offline, no account, free.

## The angle
**The sentence is ready before the bus is.** No classroom Spanish, no subscription, no account — just the exact phrase you need at the exact moment you need it. The video shows the real product loop: pick a situation, flip the card, rate it honestly. Quiet premium product film in the app's own terracotta-and-cream identity.

## Hook (first 2-3 seconds)
Full-bleed dark brown (#241510). The app's actual hero line lands in cream display type, two beats:

> Sprich Spanisch,
> **bevor der Bus kommt.**

Second line highlighted in terracotta. Small eyebrow above: `REISE-SPANISCH FÜR LATEINAMERIKA`. This is the product's own strongest copy — no invented tagline.

## Key moments (the middle)
- The **card flip**: front „Wie viel kostet das?" → flip → „**¿Cuánto cuesta?**" with the real pronunciation tip „KUAN-to KUES-ta". This is the product doing its thing.
- The **area chips**: Bus · Essen · Geld · Notfall — one gets selected (simulated tap). Entry point of the real user flow.
- The **honest rating row**: Nochmal · Gut · Einfach — „Gut" gets tapped; the app schedules the rest (SRS).
- One restrained fact line: **2.293 Karten · 72 Bereiche · offline · 0 €** (the landing page's real trust numbers).

## Outro / punchline
The gradient map-pin logo (terracotta → amber, with „¿?") scales in softly. „HolaRuta" in display type, tagline below: „Reise-Spanisch für echte Situationen." Then, small: `holaruta.com`. Music fades. Silence.

## User flow worth showing
1. **Entry** — choose an area chip (Geld) from Bus · Essen · Geld · Notfall.
2. **Key action** — flashcard front „Wie viel kostet das?", card flips to „¿Cuánto cuesta?" + pronunciation tip.
3. **Result** — rate it: Nochmal / Gut / Einfach; „Gut" tapped, subtle confirm. The app plans the next review.

This flow is the centerpiece (scenes 2–3). The hero copy frames it; it does not replace it.

## Tone
- Preset: polished
- Creative direction: quiet premium product film — warm terracotta identity, confidence through restraint
- Interpretation: 4 scenes, longer holds, soft crossfades (0.6–0.8s), no aggressive type, the product's own copy does the talking.
